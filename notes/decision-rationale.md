# Decision Rationale

- A homogeneous workstation model was preferred in order to simplify support, maintenance, and lifecycle management.
- The processor choice prioritized multithreaded performance and virtualization capability instead of unnecessary graphics investment.
- Integrated graphics were considered sufficient for the intended lab scenario, allowing budget allocation to RAM, storage, and power quality.
- 32 GB of RAM was treated as a core requirement to support simultaneous host and virtual machine workloads.
- NVMe storage was selected to improve responsiveness, reduce VM latency, and support snapshot-heavy environments.
- A quality PSU with significant margin was chosen to improve stability, efficiency, and future upgrade flexibility.
- Cooling and airflow were treated as operational reliability factors, not only as aesthetic or gaming-related features.
- Budget discipline was maintained without reducing the technical viability of the proposed standard.
